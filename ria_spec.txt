1. Executive Summary

The Research Intelligence Agent (RIA) is an autonomous, AI-powered system designed to provide KLE Technological University’s leadership at every organizational level with continuous visibility into research performance, progress, and strategic positioning. The system serves the Vice Chancellor and university leadership, campus directors, deans, department heads, and individual faculty members — each seeing precisely the insights relevant to their scope of responsibility.

The RIA eliminates manual report generation, removes dependency on individuals for data compilation, and enables evidence-based decision-making at every tier of the institution. Data flows upward from individual faculty research activity, aggregating automatically through departments, campuses, and the university — while strategic targets cascade downward from institutional goals to campus and department-level expectations.



Core Design Principle

The system runs autonomously without depending on any single individual. All data acquisition, processing, analysis, and reporting are automated. Every leadership layer — from the Vice Chancellor to individual faculty — accesses insights calibrated to their role and decision-making scope. Metrics roll up from faculty to department to campus to university; targets cascade down from university to campus to department.

2.2 Organizational Scope

The system covers the complete KLE Technological University organizational structure: all campuses, all departments within each campus, and all faculty members. 3.2.3 Aggregation Logic

Every research record is linked to a faculty member through foreign keys. The system automatically computes aggregates:

Department Level: Sum/average of all faculty within the department. Example: Department publication count = sum of all faculty publications in that department.

Campus Level: Sum/average of all departments within the campus. Example: Campus h-index = weighted aggregate of department h-indices.

University Level: Sum/average of all campuses. Example: Total university funding = sum across all campuses.

These aggregations are pre-computed during the nightly processing cycle and stored as materialized views for fast dashboard loading, while also being computable on-demand for real-time queries.

3.3.2 Drill-Down Navigation

The dashboard supports seamless drill-down from any level to the level below. A Vice Chancellor viewing the university dashboard can click a campus name to see that campus’s performance, then click a department, then a faculty member — all within the same interface. Breadcrumb navigation shows the current path (e.g., University ▸ Hubballi Campus ▸ Computer Science ▸ Dr. Sharma) and allows jumping back to any level.

3.4.2 Context-Aware Query Interface

The natural language query interface is aware of the user’s role and organizational context. When a user asks a question, the system automatically scopes the response to their level:

Query Example

How It Responds by Role

"How are we doing in publications this year?"

VC sees university-wide data. Campus Director sees their campus. HoD sees their department. Faculty sees their own count vs department average.

"Which areas need attention?"

VC sees underperforming campuses/departments. Campus Director sees weak departments on their campus. HoD sees inactive faculty and at-risk projects. Faculty sees their gaps vs benchmarks.

"Compare our performance with last year"

Each level sees year-on-year comparison at their scope: university totals, campus totals, department totals, or personal metrics respectively.

"Who are the top performers?"

VC sees top campuses and departments. Campus Director sees top departments on their campus. HoD sees top faculty in their department. Faculty sees their ranking within the department.



Users can also ask cross-level questions if their access permits: a VC can ask "How is the Computer Science department at the Hubballi campus performing?" and get a detailed response, while an HoD asking about another department would see only aggregate data.

4.4 Cascading Targets

The target system works top-down: institutional targets set by the Vice Chancellor are disaggregated to campus targets (by the VC/Deans) and then to department targets (by Campus Directors/Deans). Each level sees their specific target alongside current performance. The dashboard shows green (on track), amber (at risk), and red (behind) indicators at every level against these cascaded targets.

9. Person-Independence Design

Design Element

How It Ensures Independence

Automated Data Pull

All data acquisition runs on schedules. No person manually extracts, compiles, or uploads data at any level.

Automatic Aggregation

Metrics roll up automatically from faculty through department, campus, to university. No one manually prepares campus or university reports from department data.

Self-Updating Dashboard

Every level’s dashboard refreshes automatically. The VC, campus director, HoD, and faculty all see current data by simply opening their browser.

Role-Specific AI Briefings

Each leadership level receives tailored insights generated automatically. No analyst writes these reports — the AI generates them from data.

Self-Service Query

Any authorized person at any level asks questions directly. No request queue, no waiting for someone to run a report.

Cascading Targets

Once set, targets propagate automatically through the hierarchy. Progress is tracked and alerts triggered without manual monitoring.

Documented System

Complete documentation ensures any developer can maintain or extend the system. No single person holds critical knowledge.



Ongoing human tasks are limited to: adding Scopus IDs for new faculty, updating the organizational hierarchy when departments or campuses change, periodic server maintenance, and annual review of targets and alert thresholds.



10. Risks & Mitigation

Risk

Impact

Mitigation

Incomplete Scopus Coverage

Faculty without Scopus profiles lead to undercounting, distorting department and campus metrics

Supplement with Google Scholar; institutional drive to ensure all faculty have Scopus profiles; track coverage percentage per department

Organizational Hierarchy Changes

Departments merging, new campuses, faculty transfers could break aggregation

Flexible hierarchy model with effective dates; admin interface for updating structure without developer intervention

Internal Data Quality

Inconsistent funded project or PhD records across departments/campuses

Standard data format enforced at entry; validation rules; data quality score per department visible to campus directors

Multi-Level Complexity

Four dashboard levels increase development time and testing effort

Phased rollout: university+department first, campus and faculty views added in Phase 2; reusable component architecture

User Adoption Across Levels

Different user sophistication levels; faculty may not use the system

Role-specific onboarding; proactive email briefings don’t require login; simple, clean interface design; mobile access

AI Hallucination

AI-generated insights may be inaccurate, especially at granular levels

Always pass actual data; include source references; level-specific prompt engineering with guardrails

Privacy Concerns

Faculty may object to individual-level performance visibility

Strict access control: only HoD sees individual faculty data; faculty see only their own profile; clear data usage policy communicated upfront



11. Success Criteria

Multi-Level Data Completeness: 90%+ of active faculty publications captured, with correct department and campus attribution.

Dashboard Uptime: 99% availability during working hours across all levels.

Adoption Across Levels: Within first quarter: VC/Pro-VC regularly use university view; at least 3 Campus Directors use campus view; at least 50% of HoDs use department view.

Aggregation Accuracy: University and campus aggregate numbers match independently verified totals with less than 2% variance.

AI Briefing Relevance: 80%+ of recipients at each level rate their briefings as useful or very useful.

Strategic Impact: At least 3 strategic decisions at university or campus level informed by RIA insights within 6 months.

Person-Independence: System updates all levels and generates all briefings for 4+ consecutive weeks without manual intervention.

Query Quality: Context-aware queries return role-appropriate, data-backed responses 85%+ of the time.
